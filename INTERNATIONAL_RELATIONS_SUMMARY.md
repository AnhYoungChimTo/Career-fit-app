## üìö Key Characteristics by Sector

### NGO Sector
- **Focus:** Program management, community development, grassroots impact
- **Strengths:** High impact, mission-driven, diverse roles
- **Challenges:** Lower salaries (vs diplomacy/UN), high workload
- **Best for:** Those who value impact over income, hands-on program work

### Diplomacy & Government
- **Focus:** Bilateral relations, protocol, policy representation
- **Strengths:** Prestige, high salaries, travel, influence
- **Challenges:** Very high competition, long career progression, high stress
- **Best for:** Those with strong academic background, language skills, patience for hierarchy

### International Development (UN/Multilateral)
- **Focus:** SDGs, policy coordination, large-scale programs
- **Strengths:** Highest salaries, global impact, stability
- **Challenges:** Competitive entry (especially JPO), bureaucratic
- **Best for:** Policy-minded individuals with technical expertise

### Humanitarian & Emergency
- **Focus:** Crisis response, emergency relief, saving lives
- **Strengths:** Highest impact, adrenaline, rapid problem-solving
- **Challenges:** Extremely stressful, poor work-life balance, security risks
- **Best for:** Resilient individuals who thrive in chaos and uncertainty

### Policy, Research & Advocacy
- **Focus:** Evidence generation, policy influence, campaigns
- **Strengths:** Intellectual work, better work-life balance, thought leadership
- **Challenges:** Less direct impact visibility, academia-like competition
- **Best for:** Analytical thinkers, writers, researchers
